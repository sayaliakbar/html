Exercise 1: Evaluating Accessibility Issues

Introduction

In this exercise, you will practice identifying and evaluating common accessibility issues. Learning to recognize these problems is the first step toward creating more accessible websites.

For each example below:

  1. Identify the accessibility issues
  2. Explain why they are problematic
  3. Suggest how to fix them

Task 3: Color and Contrast

Examine this example where color is used to convey information:

Order Status

Check the status of your orders below.

  • Order #12345
  • Order #12346
  • Order #12347

Updated 5 minutes ago

Questions:

  1. What accessibility issues related to color and contrast can you identify?
  2. How would users with color blindness or low vision experience this content?
  3. What changes would improve the accessibility?

Your Analysis:

Tip: Remember WCAG guidelines recommend a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. Also, color should never be the only means of conveying information.

Task 4: Document Structure

Review this HTML structure:

<div class="header">Site Title</div>
<div class="nav">
  <div><a href="#">Home</a></div>
  <div><a href="#">Products</a></div>
  <div><a href="#">Contact</a></div>
</div>

<div class="main">
  <div class="title">Welcome to Our Website</div>
  
  <div class="content">
    <div class="heading">About Our Products</div>
    <div>We offer a wide range of products...</div>
    
    <div class="heading">Latest Releases</div>
    <div>Check out our newest items...</div>
  </div>
  
  <div class="sidebar">
    <div class="heading">Recent Posts</div>
    <div>Post 1...</div>
    <div>Post 2...</div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="footer">Copyright 2023</div>

Questions:

  1. What structural accessibility issues can you identify in this HTML?
  2. How would screen reader users experience this document structure?
  3. What changes would improve the document's accessibility?

Your Analysis:

Tip: Consider semantic HTML elements, heading hierarchy, and how the document would be navigated using assistive technologies.
